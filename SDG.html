SDG-2

Sustainable Development Goal 2 (SDG 2) aims to achieve “zero hunger.”
It is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals established by the United Nations in 2015.
The official wording for SDG 2 is: “End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture

  1. Zero Hunger by 2030: The primary objective of SDG 2 is to create a world free of hunger by the year 2030.
    Unfortunately, the global issue of hunger and food insecurity has been on the rise since 2015.
    Factors such as the pandemic, conflict, climate change, and deepening inequalities have exacerbated this trend.
    As of 2022, approximately 735 million people (about 9.2% of the world’s population) suffer from chronic hunger, a significant increase compared to 2019.
    Additionally, an estimated 2.4 billion people face moderate to severe food insecurity.
  2. Challenges and Impact:
    • Malnutrition: Extreme hunger and malnutrition hinder sustainable development.
      When people lack proper nourishment, they become less productive, more susceptible to diseases, and unable to improve their livelihoods.
    • Stunting and Wasting: In 2022, 148 million children had stunted growth,
      and 45 million children under the age of 5 were affected by wasting due to inadequate nutrition.
    • Projected Hunger: It is projected that more than 600 million people worldwide will face hunger by 2030,
      highlighting the immense challenge of achieving the zero hunger target.
  3. Factors Contributing to Hunger:
    • Conflict: Armed conflicts disrupt food production and distribution, leading to scarcity.
    • Climate Shocks: Extreme weather events affect crop yields and food availability.
    • Rising Food Prices: Food prices remain high in many countries, impacting affordability.
    • Declining Food Production: Decreased agricultural productivity exacerbates food scarcity.
    • Investment in Agriculture: Critical for reducing hunger, poverty, and building resilience.
  4. Why Should We Care?:
    • A world with zero hunger positively impacts economies, health, education, equality, and social development.
    • Hunger limits human development, hindering progress toward other sustainable development goals like education and gender equality.
  5. Achieving Zero Hunger:
    • Multi-Dimensional Approach: Achieving food security requires a multi-dimensional approach:
    • Social Protection: Ensuring access to food for vulnerable populations.
    • Sustainable Agriculture: Promoting practices that enhance food production while preserving the environment.
    • Transforming Food Systems: Creating a more inclusive and sustainable world.
